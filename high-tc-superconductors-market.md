<p><p>High-temperature superconductors (HTS) are categorized into two main types: 1G HTS and 2G HTS. 1G HTS refers to the first generation of superconducting materials, typically based on rare earth barium copper oxide, which are often used in applications like magnetic field generators and power cables. In contrast, 2G HTS represents the second generation, known for their higher current capacity and flexibility, enabling advanced applications in transportation, energy storage, and medical imaging technologies. Both market segments are crucial for evolving superconducting technologies.</p></p>
