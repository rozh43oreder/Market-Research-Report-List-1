<p><ul><li>Attenuated Live Vaccines</li><li>Conjugate Vaccines</li><li>Inactivated Vaccines</li><li>Subunit Vaccines</li><li>Toxoid Vaccines</li><li>DNA Vaccines</li><li>Recombinant Vaccines</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cat vaccines market comprises various types, each designed for specific immune responses. Attenuated live vaccines contain weakened pathogens, eliciting strong immunity. Inactivated vaccines use killed pathogens, ensuring safety but requiring boosters. Conjugate vaccines link antigens to enhance efficacy, while subunit vaccines use only parts of the pathogen for targeted protection. Toxoid vaccines neutralize toxins from bacteria. DNA vaccines utilize genetic material to provoke an immune response, and recombinant vaccines combine elements of pathogens for improved effectiveness and safety.</p></p>
